Resources:

For those interested in learning more about various topics discussed in this blog, I'm compiling here a short, incomplete list of pages you might find helpful. If you have suggestions, be sure to let me know!

Particle Physics

The PDG Review of Particle Physics - an indispensible compilation of reviews on a number of particle physics topics along with up to date data on the standard model particles and some common extensions.

Of Particular Significance - Matt Strassler's site is somewhere between a blog and a resource, but he has a number of articles with pedagogical introductions to modern topics in particle physics.

The ATLAS and CMS public results pages have up to date information from their respective experiments. Some of the biggest results from ATLAS are also summarized in their Physics Briefings.

ParticleBites features summaries of particle physics research papers and recent news written by graduate students at a relatively accessible level. (See also their sister site, astrobites.)

General Physics

Physics by APS offers brief summaries of some of the most significant papers published in the APS journals at a much more accessible level.
